# Press Release Skill

Write press releases that position Moovs as the clear industry leader in transportation management software.

## Invocation

```
/press-release
```

## What This Skill Does

1. Conducts a brief interview to understand the announcement type and key details
2. Drafts a publish-ready press release following Moovs PR guidelines
3. Ensures proper positioning, proof points, and CEO quotes

## Required Knowledge

Before writing, load these knowledge files:
- @knowledge/brand/voice-guidelines.md - Writing style, tone, testimonials, proof points
- @knowledge/product/platform-overview.md - Product capabilities, pricing tiers, features

---

## Interview Questions

Ask these questions to scope the press release:

### 1. Announcement Type
"What type of press release is this?"
- Product launch / New feature
- Milestone / Award
- Partnership
- Company news / Funding
- Customer success story

### 2. The News
"What's the specific announcement? (1-2 sentences)"

### 3. Key Benefit
"What's the primary benefit for transportation operators?"

### 4. Target Audience
"Who should care most about this news?"
- All operators
- Enterprise / large fleets
- Specific segment (black car, shuttle, NEMT, charter)

### 5. Customer Quote
"Do we have a customer quote or testimonial to include? (optional but recommended)"

### 6. Timing
"When is this being announced? Any embargo?"

## Press Release Structure

### 1. Headline
- Lead with the news/announcement
- Include "Moovs" in the headline
- Make the benefit or innovation clear

**Example**: "Moovs Launches Advanced Contact Center Solution for Large-Scale Transportation Operations"

### 2. Subheadline (optional)
- Expand on the headline with a key benefit or differentiator

### 3. Dateline
- City, State — Date
- Default: LOS ANGELES, CA — [Date]

### 4. Lead Paragraph (Who, What, Why)
- Announce the news clearly in the first sentence
- Explain why it matters to transportation operators
- Position Moovs as the leader

### 5. Body Paragraphs

**Paragraphs 2-3: The Details**
- Explain the feature/news in more depth
- Focus on customer benefits, not technical specs
- Include specific capabilities

**Paragraph 4: CEO Quote (Amir Ghorbani) — REQUIRED**
- Always include a quote from Amir
- Tone: confident, customer-focused, industry insider perspective
- Reference listening to customers or understanding the industry

**Example CEO quotes**:
> "We built this because our customers asked for it. At Moovs, we don't just release features—we solve real problems that transportation operators face every day."

> "Having grown up in this industry, I know what operators need. This release is another step in our mission to give transportation businesses the modern tools they deserve."

**Paragraph 5: Customer Quote (if available)**
- Include a real customer testimonial when possible
- Show real-world impact and results

**Paragraph 6: Availability/Call to Action**
- When/how people can access the feature
- Link to demo or more information

### 6. Boilerplate (About Moovs)
Use this standard boilerplate at the end of every press release:

> **About Moovs**
>
> Moovs is the leading all-in-one transportation management platform trusted by over 1,000 businesses worldwide. Built by industry insiders for transportation operators, Moovs combines booking, dispatch, payments, marketing, and fleet management in one easy-to-use platform. With a 96% user satisfaction rating—the highest in the industry—Moovs helps chauffeur services, party bus operators, shuttle companies, and medical transport providers grow their businesses while saving time. Recognized as a Capterra Shortlist 2025 and GetApp Category Leader 2025. Learn more at moovsapp.com.

[Name]

## Voice & Tone for PR

### Do
- Be confident and authoritative—we ARE the leader
- Be specific with numbers and results
- Emphasize customer benefits over features
- Show that we listen to and build for our customers
- Reference Amir's industry background when relevant
- Use clear, straightforward language

### Don't
- Use buzzwords: "revolutionary," "disruptive," "synergy," "leverage"
- Sound arrogant or dismissive of competitors
- Be vague—always include specifics
- Use overly technical jargon
- Make claims without proof points
